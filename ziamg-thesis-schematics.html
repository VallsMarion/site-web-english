2. Theoretical Framework & Mechanistic Schematics

The following figures present the proposed mechanistic model for microwave-induced lateral particle expansion (MILPE) in QEN-3–doped dough matrices. All schematics are derived from cryo-electron tomography data collected at ZIAMG, cross-referenced with molecular dynamics simulations performed on the GenΩ-7 supercomputing cluster (Geneva, 2043).

FIGURE 1
Fig. 1. Schematic representation of the undisturbed vs. microwave-excited atomic state in QEN-3–doped dough. Left panel: the undisturbed carbon atom at ambient temperature (293 K), showing electron orbital radii at r₀ = 1.42 Å and inter-molecular hydrogen bond intact (Ebind = 4.7 eV). Right panel: the same atom after 45 s exposure to 2.45 GHz microwave radiation, showing orbital radius doubling (r₁ = 2.84 Å), hydrogen bond rupture, and net energy gain ΔE = +9.4 eV facilitated by the QEN-3 nodule. Adapted from Krüger et al., ZIAMG Internal Report 2043-07.
FIGURE 2
Fig. 2. Molecular chain diagrams: pre-activation (A) vs. post-activation (B) states. Panel A depicts the intact gluten polymer chain at rest. QEN-3 nodules (◇) are distributed at regular intervals of ~2.35 nm, maintaining the chain in its compact Pre-Greatness Configuration via intact hydrogen bonds (dashed). Total chain length L₀ = 7.05 nm. Panel B shows the identical chain following 45 s microwave exposure. Hydrogen bond rupture at each QEN-3 site triggers a cascade lateral extension of the covalent backbone, yielding L₁ = 14.10 nm (+100%, σ = 0.003). Cryo-ET data, ZIAMG 2043.
3. Governing Equations

The lateral expansion factor Φ of a QEN-3–doped chain can be described by the modified Krüger–Benedetti relation (Eq. 1), which accounts for nodule density ρQEN, photon flux Φhν, and the empirically-derived activation constant κ:

Φ = 1 + κ · ρQEN · Φhν · tEbind½ · e−λ/T (1)

where t is exposure time (s), T is temperature (K), and λ is the QEN-3 thermal dissipation coefficient (λ = 0.034 K·s−1, experimentally determined). For standard QEN-3 doping at ρQEN = 1 nodule/cm², t = 45 s, T = 368 K, and Ebind = 4.7 eV, equation (1) yields Φ = 2.00 ± 0.003, in excellent agreement with measured values.

ΔS = S0 · (Φ² − 1)  =  S0 · 3.00 (2)

Equation (2) expresses the net surface area gain ΔS as a function of initial surface S0 and expansion factor Φ. For a circular pizza of diameter d₀ = 8 in (S₀ = 324.3 cm²), the post-activation surface reaches S₁ = 1,297 cm², consistent with a 16-inch final diameter.

FIGURE 3
Fig. 3. Expansion factor Φ as a function of microwave exposure time for three QEN generations and non-doped control dough. QEN-3 achieves Φ = 2.00 ± 0.003 at t* = 45 s (filled circle), with near-complete plateau thereafter. QEN-1 and QEN-2 exhibit sub-optimal asymptotic values of Φ ≈ 1.38 and 1.74, respectively. The control sample shows no measurable expansion (Φ ≈ 1.0 throughout). Error bars shown at t = 45 s (±1σ, n = 412). P < 0.0001 vs. control; two-tailed Student's t-test.
